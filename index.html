Case study · E-commerce · Mobile

Rakuten Ichiba Cart Redesign

A mobile-first redesign of Japan’s most fragmented multi-merchant checkout. I reframed the cart as a decision-making space and built five strategic pillars — cost transparency, streamlined guest checkout, mobile-first responsiveness, personalization, and trust signals — mapped directly to Baymard abandonment data. The result targets a measurable 2–5 point reduction in mobile cart abandonment.

Case study · Fintech · Wireframing

Hinode Bank — Mobile Banking Wireframes

A low-fidelity Balsamiq prototype for a mobile banking app, designed around the three needs finance users care about most: confidence, clarity, and control. Eight connected screens and a full navigation map carry a user from secure login through a reviewed, biometric-confirmed money transfer, with every interactive element annotated to the screen it links to.

About

A designer who starts with evidence and ends with clarity.

I am a Product Designer and UX Designer based in Yokohama, Japan, and a graduate student in User Experience Design & Testing at Northeastern University. I design for the short, high-stakes moments that define whether a product feels trustworthy — a gate tap during rush hour, a money transfer between meetings, a checkout on a crowded train.

My process follows Garrett’s five planes: I define strategy and scope, structure the information, shape the skeleton, and refine the surface. Along the way I lean on Norman’s pairing of affordances with visible signifiers so that nothing a user experiences happens by accident.
